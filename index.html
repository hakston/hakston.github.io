I am Akston.

Essay

I like work that holds up when you look at it closely. The fake version of care is easy to produce now. It has the right texture, the right words, the right posture. But once you lean on it, it gives way.

The real thing is plainer. A page with decent margins. A system that knows how it can fail. A sentence that says what it means and then stops. Most good work is decided there, before anyone gets tempted to decorate it.

So this page is simple on purpose. If anything ends up here, it should be because it was worth making properly.
