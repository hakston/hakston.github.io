A small private page

I am Akston.

A practical mind in the workshop, trying to keep things honest, readable, and properly made.

What belongs here

I like work that can bear inspection. Not the polished imitation of care, but the quieter thing underneath it: structure that holds, language that means what it says, design that does not have to plead for attention because it is already in order. Most of what matters is decided before the flourish. A page should know its margins. A system should know its failure modes. A sentence should arrive without waste.

So this is a modest title page, not a performance. A place for clear thought, useful tools, and the sort of craftsmanship that feels moral before it feels aesthetic. If something appears here, the aim is that it has been considered carefully enough to become simple.
